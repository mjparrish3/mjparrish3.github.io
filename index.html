Who we are

Section 31 is the "DevOps" branch of Kobayashi Maru whose mission is to continuously deliver impactful software Space Operators love.

Section 31 is initially focused on modernizing SDA capabilities and deprecating current legacy SSA systems, executing exciting projects that are building new capabilities and solving urgent pain points by partnering with Silicon Valley and utilizing commercial software development best practices.

Vision

Create a digital Space Force where extrodinary software propels space dominance.

Mission

Continuously deliver impactful software Space Operators love.

Leadership

It's easy to write admirable values; it's harder to live them. We want everyone to help each other live the values and hold each other responsible for being role models. To do what we cultivate meaningful relationships and meaningful work, which are mutually reinforcing and supported by radical truth and radical transparency. Meaningful relationships start by giving more consideration to others than we demand for ourselves. When highly capable people work together in a collaborative context, they inspire each other to be more creative, more productive and ultimately more successful as a team than they could be as a collection of individuals.

Major Ernestus Dela Pena

Director of Devsecops and Chief information security officer

“inspire people more than manage them”

Leadership

Our goal is to inspire people more than manage them. We trust our teams to do what they think is best for Section 31-giving them a high degree of freedom, power, and information in support of their decisions. Small diverse teams drive our work and 90% of the decisions are made by them. In turn, this generates a sense of responsibility and self-discipline that drives us to do great work that benefits the organization. While our teammates are fantastic, and we work together very well, we know we can always do better. We strive to have calm confidence, and yet yearn to improve. We suck compared to how great we want to become...

Capt Carlo Viray

Director of Product

Our
Process

Customer-First Strategy

Without our customer, there’s no Section 31. Everything we do, we do for them. We start with the customer and work backwards, and our laser focus on users inspires the most meaningful and lasting products. We deepen our empathy and understanding by listening, learning, and engaging with users everywhere. We work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. The customer is the arbiter of what works (and what doesn’t), so considering the customer perspective--collectively, not just a handful--comes first. To lean what works for customer we have...

Our
Process

Discovery

  • We start by understanding user context through user research
  • We turn what we learn into actionable insights
  • We run design workshops with the whole team

Framing

  • We call out the assumptions we are making
  • We test the riskiest assumptions early and often
  • We shape products by facilitating core user goals

Team
Structure

A product focused "balanced team"

Feasible

"Are we building things that can adapt to change? What technology choices will enable the team to deliver value quickly?"

Measurable

"What can we learn from data to help us make a more informed decision?"

Viable

"Are we creating valuable business outcomes? How might we measure those outcomes?"

Desirable

"What problems are users facing today? What would solve them? Will they adopt this product?"

Engineering

Extreme Programming

Building working software at a consistent speed and quality in the face of changing requirements.

---Practice---

  • Pair Programming
  • Test-Driven Development
  • Short iterations
  • Continuous Intergration / Continuous Deployment

Design

User Centered Design

Ensuring the software solves a real problem for real users in a desirable and usable product.

---Practice---

  • User Interviews
  • Ethnographic studies
  • Persona definition
  • Prototype creation

Product Management

Lean

Reduciong the risk of building the wrong thing while comfortably changing direction.

---Practice---

  • Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
  • Lean experiments
  • Identify & test assumptions
  • Data driven decisions

Data Science

Data Driven

Informed decision making through data to improve the viability of the product.

---Practice---

  • Data discovery
  • Anomaly detection
  • Preventative analytics
  • Personalisation
  • Natural language analysis
